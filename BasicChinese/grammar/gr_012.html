Chinese Grammar - Nominal measure words

Index
Chinese nouns, preceeded either by a number, a demonstrative pronoun, or an interrogative pronoun, require a measure word before the noun. Some measure words have an English equivalent, e.g.

一束花 yī shù huā a bunch of flowers
一群羊 yī qún yáng a flock of sheep

Most Chinese measure words have, however, no English equivalent, e.g.三个人 (sān gè rén) three people
The most common and generic measure word is 个 (gè). It is used with most abstract terms.
Some measure words indicate a quantity and have an English equivalent:

两瓶啤酒 liǎng píng píjiǔ two bottles of beer
一副手套 yī fù shǒutào a pair of gloves
一块蛋糕 yī kuài dàngāo a slice of cake
一张纸 yī zhāng zhǐ a piece of paper

Some measure words are used with nouns relating to common semantic features:

张 (flat things): 一张桌子 yī zhāng zhuōzi a table
本 (books): 一本词典 yī běn cídiǎn a dictionary
条 (winding things): 一条裤子,一条河 yī tiáo kùzi, yī tiáo hé a pair of trousers, a river
篇 (paper work, documents): 一篇文章 yī piān wénzhāng an essay
把 (something with a handle): 一把椅子, 一把雨伞 yī bǎ yǐzi, yī bǎ yǔsǎn a chair, an umbrella

Some measure words are very specific to a particular noun 一匹马 (yī pǐ mǎ) a horse

Examples:

三个老师 这个老师 哪个老师 六匹马 这 本书 哪把椅子
sān gè lǎoshī zhè gè lǎoshī nǎ gè lǎoshī liù pǐ mǎ zhè běn shū nǎ bǎ yǐzi
three teachers this teacher what teacher? six horses this book which chair?

Common measure words:

个 gè general 把 bǎ things with handles 杯 bēi cups, glasses 本 běn books, maps
件 jiàn clothes 封 fēng letters 架 jià airplanes, machines 间 jiān rooms
篇 piān documents 棵 kē plants 块 kuài cakes 辆 liàng cars
瓶 píng liquid 位 wèi person (more
formal than 个)		 只 zhī animals 座 zuò buildings, mountains
