Chinese Grammar - Passive

Index
The passive is usually implied by context. Under the influence of Western languages overt passive constructions have become more common recently. The logical subject of the passive construction is marked by 被 (bèi). In spoken Chinese 让 (ràng), 叫 (jiào) or 给 (gěi) are used as well.

我的笔被她拿走了。 wǒde bǐ bèi tā ná zǒu le My pen was taken away by her.
我的书让我朋友借了。 wǒde shū ràng wǒ péngyou jiè le My book was borrowed by my friend.


If the logical subject is obvious from the context it can be dropped.

他的自行车被偷了。 tāde zìxíngchē bèi tōu le His bicycle was stolen.
