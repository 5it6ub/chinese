Dates

The year is pronounced as a series of digits (not as a single number) followed by 年 (nián):
2008: 二零零八年 (èrlínglíngbā nián)
In a date the order is always from the largest to the smallest unit:
4.6.2006: 二零零六年六月四日 (èrlínglíngliù nián liùyuè sì rì)
NB: the day is followed by 日 (rì) or 号 (hào).

If a time is given, the time follows the date:
奥运会二零零八年八月八日晚上八点开始了。
àoyùnhuì èrlínglíngbā nián bāyuè bā rì wǎnshang bā diǎn kāishǐ le.
The Olympic Games started at eight o'clock of the evening of 8. August 2008.
For AD use: 公元 (gōngyuán)
For BC use: 公元前 (gōngyuánqián)
