Small Numbers

The basic numbers in Chinese are:
0 零,〇 líng   6 六 liù
1 一 yī 7 七 qī
2 二 èr 8 八 bā
3 三 sān 9 九 jiǔ
4 四 sì 10 十 shí
5 五 wǔ  
On cheques and other important documents these numbers are often written in a more complex form:
壹 (1) 贰 (2) 叁 (3) 肆 (4) 伍 (5) 陆 (6) 柒 (7) 捌 (8) 玖 (9) 拾 (10)
The numbers 11 to 19 are formed by adding the numbers 1 to 9 to 十:
十一, 十二, 十三 ... 十九 (shíyī, shíèr, shísān ... shíjiǔ)
The numbers twenty to ninety are formed by adding the numbers 1 to 9 in front of 十:
二十,三十 ... 九十 (èrshí, sānshí ... jiǔshí)
Any numbers in between are formed by adding the tens in front of 十 and ones after 十.
Examples:
24: 二十四 èrshísì   38: 三十八 sānshíbā
56: 五十六 wǔshíliù   83: 八十三 bāshísān
99: 九十九 jiǔshíjiǔ   The number 100 is 百 (bǎi).
