Enumeration

Chinese traditionally uses a enumeration system consisting of two cycles: the ten heavenly stems and the twelve earthly branches.

The heavenly stems are:
甲乙 丙丁 戊己 庚辛 壬癸
jiǎyǐ bǐngdīng wùjǐ gēngxīn rénguǐ

The heavenly stems are often used to enumerate items in a series, e.g. the contents of a book or the anonymous speakers in a dialogue.


The earthly branches are:
子丑 寅卯 晨巳 午未 申酉 戌亥
zǐchóu yínmǎo chénsì wǔwèi shēnyǒu xūhài

The heavenly stems and earthly branches are combined into a 60 step cycle which is traditionally used to name the years in the Chinese calender.
