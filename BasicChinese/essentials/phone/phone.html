Phone

A telephone is called 电话 (diànhuà) and to make a call to somebody is 打给X一个电话 (dǎ gěi X yi ge diànhuà).
The telephone number is 电话号码 (diànhuà hàomǎ).
A mobile phone is called 手提 (shǒutí).
A fax machine is called 传真 (chuānzhen).

The usual way of saying 'hello' on the phone is 喂 (wèi).

Phone numbers are normally given as a series of digits, 幺 (yāo) is often used instead of 一 'yī' to avoid any confusion with 七 (qī).

To ask for a phone number you say:
你的电话号码是多少 ? (nǐ de diànhuà hàomǎ shì duōshǎo).
Lit. Your phone number is how much?

To ask if somebody is available ask:   X 在吗? (X zài ma)
The answer is likely to be:
在. 请稍等. (zài qǐng shāoděng) He/she is. Please wait a moment.
or   不在.(bù zài) He/she is not.

To ask somebody to ring you back, you can say:
请给我会(个)电话. (qǐng gěi wǒ huì (ge) diànhuà) - Please all me back.
(Note the measure word ge is optional in this phrase)

Instead of 请稍等 you can also hear 请等一等 (qǐng děng yī děng) - Please wait a little.
