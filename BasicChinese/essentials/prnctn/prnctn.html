Introduction

This section provides a brief introduction to the pronunciation of Mandarin Chinese. It uses Pinyin, the modern standard system of romanization adopted by the People's Republic of China, to transcribe the pronunciation of Chinese. The main rationale for the introduction of Pinyin was to help Chinese children to associate Chinese characters with their standard pronunciation, support for speakers of others languages was only a secondary consideration.

Chinese syllables are made up of an initial and a final element, or a final element on its own. In addition each syllable has one of five tones.

Pinyin has some spelling rules that affect the the link between the written Pinyin and the actual pronunciation fo some syllables.

Some Chinese sounds are particulary difficult to pronounce or their association with Pinyin appears unusual to speakers of most Western languages.

Finally, there is some information on how the Pinyin and Wade-Giles system of romanization relate to each other.
