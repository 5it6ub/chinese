Wade-Giles

The Wade-Giles system of romanization was invented by Thomas Francis Wade and Herbert Allan Giles in the second half of the 19th and early 20th century. It was widely used until it began to be replaced by Pinyin in the latter half of the twentieth century. It is still in wide use in Taiwan and most older books about China use Wade-Giles or a close equivalent to represent Chinese words, especially the names of places and people.

The major differences are:
WG marks aspiration with a hyphen after the consonant, Pinyin uses different letters: t and t' vs. d and t. p and p' vs. b p.
WG uses hs for Pinyin x and j for Pinyin r.
