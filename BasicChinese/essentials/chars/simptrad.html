Simplified/Traditional

For some time efforts have been made to simplify Chinese characters. In the 1960 the People's Republic of China adopted a system of simplified characters developed over the preceeding years. The simplification was mainly achieved by reducing the number of strokes and sometimes replacing one component with a simpler one. Occasionally, this has led to the introduction of some ambiguity when one simplified character represents two different traditional characters.

Here are some of the principles used for simplifying characters:

Simplify the shape: 风 → 風
Change the phonetic component to a simpler one: 极 → 極
Omit some components: 宁 → 寧
Simplify commmon components, particularly radicals: 门 → 門,問,閉
