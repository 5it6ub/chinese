Forms of address

Index
The general form of address is the person's surname followed by his or her title.
The generic titles are:
先生 xiānsheng 王先生 Mr Wang 太太 tàitai 王太太 Mrs Wang
女士 nǚshì 王女士 Ms Wang (formal) 小姐 xiǎojiě 王小姐 Miss Wang

It is common to add the job title after a person's name. The job title can also be used as a form of address by itself. Common titles are:
老师 lǎoshī 王老师 Teacher Wang
师傅 shīfu 王师傅 Master Wang (used for craftsman, taxidrivers etc.)
经理 jīnglǐ 王经理 Manager Wang
教授 jiàoshòu 王教授 Professor Wang
秘书 mìshu 王秘书 Secretary Wang

Older people are often addressed informally as:
阿姨 āyí aunty 伯父 bófù uncle

When addressing friends it is common to use their surname preceeded by 小 or 老 depending on their age relative to yours.
小 xiǎo 小王(young) Wang 老 lǎo 老王(old) Wang
